Brand Vision

GOEYE should not be viewed as just another eyewear brand. My goal is for it to become an elevated lifestyle movement with genuine cultural impact — a name people integrate seamlessly into their daily lives, one that sets trends on major runways, is worn by top models, and carries prestige that extends well beyond the fashion industry. We are not simply selling glasses; we are building an iconic identity.

To scale this vision, our Instagram strategy needs a precise, high-energy balance between premium aesthetics and indirect, narrative-driven marketing. Rather than relying on aggressive, repetitive direct ads that dilute brand value, the focus should be on high-retention storytelling, elite collaborations, and compelling daily content that keeps audiences hooked. The objective is a page that naturally embeds GOEYE into the lifestyle of our audience — a brand they respect, remember, and engage with every day.

Instagram Audit Findings

What's Working

1. A Wide, Clearly Showcased Product Portfolio

GOEYE's Instagram effectively showcases a diverse range of eyewear, including prescription glasses, sunglasses, and sports and performance eyewear. This broad product mix positions the brand as a comprehensive eyewear destination rather than a niche seller, allowing it to appeal to multiple customer segments with different needs and lifestyles.

2. A Strong Myth-Busting Content Angle

Messaging such as “Carrots fix your vision. Dim light ruins your eyes. Glasses make you dependent — most of what you've heard is wrong” is confident and scroll-stopping. This debunking format is one of the highest-retention content strategies on Instagram: GOEYE isn't just selling glasses, it's rewiring what people think they already know.

3. A Clear, Ownable Bio Positioning

GOEYE's bio doesn't read like every other eyewear seller — it stakes a claim on being the brand that tells people “what nobody else tells you” about eye health and eyewear. Very few competitors in this category (Lenskart, John Jacobs, Titan Eyeplus) are fighting for this educational, insider-knowledge ground, which gives GOEYE a distinct, ownable identity to build every future post around rather than competing purely on style or price.

Areas of Improvement

1. Build a Strong Brand Community

The page currently functions primarily as a content and product showcase. To drive long-term engagement and customer loyalty, GOEYE should invest in recurring content series, customer spotlights, interactive Stories, polls, challenges, and a branded hashtag — paired with stronger hooks and clearer CTAs throughout. The goal is to make followers feel like members of the GOEYE community, not just viewers of it.

2. Optimize Profile & Content Fundamentals

The profile can be strengthened with a clearer value proposition and a stronger call-to-action in the bio. Captions should carry a more consistent brand voice and structured storytelling rather than feeling generic, and hashtags should be more strategically selected for discoverability. Highlights can also be reorganized into clear categories — Reviews, Best Sellers, Sports Collection, Eye Care Tips, FAQs, and Customer Experiences — to improve navigation and build trust.

3. Shift from Direct Selling to Storytelling

The brand's premium positioning is a strong foundation, but Instagram rewards content that entertains before it sells. Rather than leading with product showcases, GOEYE should build storytelling-led content — humorous everyday situations, movie and pop-culture-inspired concepts, relatable relationship scenarios, and lifestyle narratives — where the eyewear becomes a natural part of the story. This builds stronger brand recall while protecting the brand's premium image.

4. Create a Consistent Visual Identity

Content currently varies in visual style across posts. Introducing a consistent colour grade (LUT), editing style, typography, and thumbnail design would make the feed instantly recognizable and reinforce GOEYE's premium brand identity.

5. Maintain a More Structured Posting Strategy

While GOEYE is active on Instagram, a more structured content schedule built around recurring pillars and weekly series would build audience anticipation and improve consistency. A planned content rhythm makes the brand more memorable and encourages repeat engagement — the calendar in the following section is designed to put this into practice.

9-Day Content Calendar

The calendar below is built around one motto: engagement. Every hook is designed to stop the scroll, and every CTA is designed to earn a comment, save, or share — not just a like. A Reel runs every day, since Reels are the strongest reach driver on the platform; a Carousel is added only on days where the format genuinely earns its place — educational, reference, or recap content that benefits from being saved and revisited — rather than as a default for every post.

Note on format discipline: Carousels appear only on Days 1, 4, 6, and 9 — the days where content is reference-based (myths, styling guide, product showcase, reviews) and genuinely benefits from being saved and revisited. On the remaining days, the Reel alone carries the engagement goal, since a second post would split attention rather than add to it.

Why “Main Vaapas Aaunga” Spans Two Days

Day 6 (Sports, told as an AI-generated film) and Day 8 (Metro, told as a live-shot narrative) are written as two chapters of the same idea, tied together by one line: “Har 'Main vaapas aaunga' ka reason alag hota hai” (“Every 'I'll come back' has a different reason”). Day 6 uses the line for protection, Day 8 uses it for love — spacing them two days apart lets each one land as its own story while the second post quietly rewards anyone who remembers the line from the first. The Metro story carries Day 8 specifically because it draws on a currently trending dialogue format and has the broadest emotional pull of the two, ahead of the Obsession-trend concept, which is a slightly older reference already used earlier in the week on Day 2.

Why Day 6 Is Told With AI-Generated Video

Day 6 swaps a live shoot for an AI-generated cinematic film for two reasons. First, it lets GOEYE create visuals — shifting weather, dreamlike light, a sense of motion through impossible terrain — that a real cycling shoot couldn't deliver on this budget, giving the brand a genuinely premium, almost film-trailer look for a fraction of the cost. Second, it signals that GOEYE is a brand willing to experiment with new tools the same way it asks its audience to think differently about eye care — which fits naturally with a brand built on “telling people what nobody else tells you.”

Why Two Influencer Collabs This Cycle

Day 5 and Day 7 are deliberately spaced two days apart and built around very different emotional registers — a playful couple's GRWM versus a wanderlust travel diary — so they don't compete for the same attention or feel repetitive back to back. Travel content in particular is one of the fastest-growing categories on the platform right now, and pairing it with the brand's own “See the World Through GOEYE” positioning means Day 7 isn't just a one-off influencer post — it's a preview of a bigger campaign idea the brand can keep returning to.

Influencer Collaboration Strategy

Sourcing Approach

The plan is to reach out to as many relevant micro-influencers as possible across niches where eyewear is a genuine, daily part of life — starting with optometrists and eye-care professionals, since their endorsement carries a credibility no other niche can replicate, followed by fashion, travel, editing/tech, and sports. These creators live in glasses, and their audiences already have a real, daily relationship with eyewear — which makes the partnership feel native rather than forced.

Creators will be identified based on the specific campaign objective, then sourced through targeted keyword searches, by exploring the following lists of relevant accounts, and through influencer discovery platforms. Shortlisting will be based on audience retention, comment quality, and a review of past content — specifically how premium and well-produced their work looks — along with average likes. Priority will go to creators who genuinely wear glasses themselves or have an authentic eye-care story to tell, since that authenticity is what makes the integration credible.

Flagship Campaign Concepts

“India's Unsung Visionaries”

Core idea: feature real Indians whose vision — in every sense — has quietly built this country. A farmer who reads the weather by eye. A textile worker who still threads a needle at sixty. A student cracking the UPSC exam through thick glasses. These are people whose vision, literal and figurative, has shaped everyday India.

“Tiranga Through My Eyes”

Core idea: what does Independence Day look like through the eyes of different Indians, across different cities, ages, and professions?

Travel, fashion, and sports influencers each shoot their Independence Day morning POV — literally through their glasses, or wearing GOEYE frames

Edited together as a single collaborative reel or a connected series

Tagline: “Same flag. Different eyes. One India.”

“See the World Through GOEYE”

Core idea: hand someone a pair of GOEYE eyewear and a single instruction — “This is our GOEYE pair. Show us your day through it.” No script, no brief beyond that. What comes back is real life as each person actually sees it — and that's the entire campaign.

The reason this works is that GOEYE makes a different promise to almost everyone who wears it. A grandparent's day through GOEYE looks like reading the newspaper without squinting for the first time in years. A Gen Z creator's day looks like a frame that survives three outfit changes and still photographs well at 1 AM. A delivery rider's or office commuter's day looks like glare cutting out on a highway at noon. A student's day looks like four hours of revision without the dull headache that used to follow. Each of these is a completely different relationship with the same product — and the campaign's job is simply to let every one of them be filmed in their own voice, rather than have GOEYE narrate it for them.

How it rolls out

Seed the pair across a deliberately wide spread of people — an elderly relative, a Gen Z student, an office commuter, a traveler, a video editor — so the resulting content naturally avoids looking like one repeated influencer format

Give each person nothing but the prompt — their day, their pace, their camera roll — so the footage stays unmistakably real rather than styled to look real

Compile the strongest clips into a single anthem film — quick cuts across every persona, no dialogue needed, closing on the line: “Same pair. A hundred different days.”

Turn it into an ongoing format, not a one-off: a new “through GOEYE” submission featured weekly, so the campaign keeps collecting fresh, unscripted perspectives long after launch week

What makes this more than a UGC gimmick is that it's the brand's positioning made literal: GOEYE already talks about “what nobody tells you” about eye care — this campaign extends that into “what nobody shows you” about how differently the same product fits into completely different lives. It builds brand recall without a single discount mentioned, which is exactly the kind of recall that compounds over time instead of fading the moment the offer ends.

Execution Safeguards & Refinements

The sections above set the creative and strategic direction. The fixes below close the gaps between that direction and what actually gets executed and measured — closing the premium-positioning gap, building in measurement and attribution, vetting creators properly, and giving the team budgets, templates, and guardrails instead of just ideas.

1. Premium Positioning: Closing the Brand-Value Gap

Storytelling-led content only protects premium positioning if pricing language and product proof points stay consistent everywhere else. Feed captions should drop price-first, discount-led language entirely; offers belong in Stories and paid ads, not the main grid. A recurring “Craft & Tech” highlight and three product deep-dive Reels (lens lab, materials, fit-test) give the premium claim something tangible to point to, rather than relying on tone alone.

2. KPI Baselines & Attribution

Targets only mean something against a baseline. Before the campaign starts, GOEYE should pull a 30-day historical snapshot — current followers, average Reel views, and site conversion rate — so every KPI in this plan reads as current baseline vs. campaign target, not just an isolated number. Attribution should be built in from day one: a unique UTM and a lightweight promo code per creator, and a dedicated landing page for the campaign, so traffic and conversions can be traced back to the specific creator and post that drove them.

3. Creator Vetting: Authenticity Over Follower Count

Follower-band sourcing alone doesn't catch bot-inflated audiences or low real engagement. Every shortlisted creator should clear a simple qualification checklist before outreach turns into a brief:

Recent organic content actually wearing glasses (not just a one-off sponsored post elsewhere)

Engagement quality — a quick read of comment sentiment, not just the like count

Retention pattern on 2–3 past Reels, where available

A track record of driving traffic — screenshots of past link/swipe-up results, if they have them

For any niche that's new to GOEYE, run a small paid test with one or two creators and tight UTM tracking before committing to the full roster size in that niche.

4. AI-Generated Content: Guardrails for Day 6

The AI-generated film for Day 6 is the right creative call for the budget, but it needs the same discipline as a live shoot. It should carry a visible “Created with GOEYE Studio” disclosure, follow a one-page AI style guide (LUT, mood, motion language) so it doesn't drift from the rest of the feed's visual identity, and ship alongside at least one real-shot cutdown so the format never becomes the entire visual language of the brand. Any AI asset going into paid amplification gets a quick legal check first, given the fast-moving rules around AI disclosure and likeness.

5. Community Mechanics: From Hashtag to Habit

A branded hashtag and occasional UGC reels aren't enough to build a sticky community on their own — they need a repeatable rhythm people can show up for. Three recurring formats give the community something to return to every week and month:

Weekly “Frame Story” — short customer clips submitted for a chance to be featured + a small discount, run every week without exception

Monthly “Ask the Optometrist” Live — a real eye-care professional answering questions live, with a CTA to book a free lens check (and capture an email in the process)

Quarterly city takeovers — small micro-influencer meetups in 1–2 cities for photo-ops and organic content seeding

A small recurring reward pool (featured spot + discount) is enough to kickstart participation without turning the community into a giveaway account.

6. Visual Identity: From Recommendation to Enforced Spec

“Consistent LUT and typography” only holds if it's written down somewhere the content team actually uses. A one-page visual bible — hex codes, the LUT preset itself, three approved fonts, Reel thumbnail templates, and a five-item content QA checklist — should sit alongside this plan, with a short workshop to walk the team through it and a lightweight approval step (even a simple shared checklist) before anything posts. Without that enforcement step, visual drift creeps back in within a few weeks.

7. Experimentation & Paid Amplification Plan

The 9-day calendar gives strong starting creative, but creative needs to keep improving after it goes live. A simple 6-week test plan keeps that improvement structured: pick the top three hooks from the calendar, run two thumbnail variants and two caption variants per hook, and put 20% of the influencer budget behind boosting whichever organic Reels are already outperforming. Review checkpoints at Day 7, Day 21, and Day 42 turn those results into updated creative briefs instead of letting learnings disappear after the post goes up.

8. Caption & CTA Templates

Every caption in this plan should follow the same three-part shape, so retention and save/share behaviour stay consistent across the calendar rather than depending on whichever CTA felt right that day:

Hook — one line, first thing visible before “more”, no preamble

Body — one to two lines of story or a single useful fact, never a product pitch

CTA — one specific ask: comment a word, tag a friend, save it, or follow a link — never “buy now” in organic captions

Piloting this template for two weeks and tracking comment rate and saves against the prior two weeks gives a clean before/after read on whether it's working.

9. Flagship Campaigns: Budgets & Timelines

“India's Unsung Visionaries,” “Tiranga Through My Eyes,” and “See the World Through GOEYE” are strong enough ideas to get internal buy-in on their own, but they still need a costed brief to move from concept to greenlight. Each campaign should carry a hero-film cost estimate, an influencer-seeding budget, a paid amplification budget with CPM/CPV targets, a six-week rollout timeline with milestones, and the expected outcomes (reach, site visits, UGC submissions) that justify the spend.

10. Instagram-to-CRM: Turning Followers into Repeat Customers

Reach and awareness on their own don't build lifetime value — that needs a path off Instagram and into a channel GOEYE owns. A simple funnel closes that gap: a gated download (a styling guide) offered through a link sticker captures the opt-in, a three-email onboarding sequence (welcome, product education, social proof + a first-purchase incentive) nurtures it, and a short post-purchase series (care tips, refer-a-friend) keeps the relationship going after the sale.

Quick Wins (First 14 Days)

Run a 7-day visual clean-up where every new post follows the LUT and thumbnail template, then compare engagement before/after

Set up UTM tracking and a dedicated landing page per micro-influencer so creator-driven traffic is visible within 48 hours

Run one small creator test — three creators across three different niches, identical brief — and compare which niche converts best

Take one top-performing organic Reel and boost it with two thumbnail variants to compare retention and cost-per-acquisition
